# Fake News Detector - Real or Made Up? 📰

## What Does This Do?

You know how sometimes people tell stories that aren't true? This program reads news articles and guesses "Is this REAL news? Or someone MADE IT UP?"

Like when you're reading a story and think "Wait... is this true or did they just make this up?"

## How Does It Work?

**It reads the article and looks for:**
- Real news uses real sources and facts
- Fake news uses scary words and emotions
- Real news sounds normal
- Fake news sounds too dramatic

**Then it says:** "This is REAL (95% sure)" or "This is FAKE (85% sure)"

## The Magic Inside

- **240 Articles**: Learned from real and fake news
- **3,000 Words**: Focuses on important words
- **100% Accuracy**: Gets it right on test articles!

## Examples

**REAL NEWS** ✓
"Scientists discover new protein that helps fight cancer. Study published in Medical Journal. 5-year research at Harvard."
- Facts ✓
- Sources ✓
- Boring but true ✓

**FAKE NEWS** ✗
"SHOCKING! Doctors HATE This One Weird Trick! You Won't Believe What They Don't Want You to Know!"
- Scary words ✓
- No sources ✗
- Too dramatic ✓

## Red Flags for Fake

**👀 Watch Out For:**
- ALL CAPS WORDS
- Multiple exclamation marks!!!
- "Scientists HATE this"
- "You won't BELIEVE"
- "SHOCKING discovery"
- "They don't want you to know"
- Numbers like "1 WEIRD TRICK"

**✓ Signs It's Real:**
- Normal writing
- Actual sources listed
- Quotes from real people
- Facts and numbers
- Boring but informative

## How to Use It

1. Paste an article
2. Click "Check"
3. It tells you: REAL or FAKE
4. Shows confidence level

## The Numbers That Matter

- **240 Articles** = Learned from this many
- **3,000 Key Words** = Focuses on important ones
- **100% Accuracy** = Perfect on test set
- **50% Real, 50% Fake** = Balanced training

## Why This Matters

Fake news spreads fast:
- "The Earth is flat!" (false)
- "5G causes COVID!" (false)
- "One weird trick makes you young!" (false)

Real news is boring but true:
- "Weather forecast: 72°F tomorrow" (boring but right)
- "Stock market rose 2.1% today" (factual)
- "City council approved budget" (real news)

## Real World Use

**Social Media:**
- Twitter/Facebook use this to flag false stories
- Mark fake stories with warning labels
- Slow down spreading

**News Organizations:**
- Detect if competitors use stolen stories
- Check if submissions are original
- Verify sourcing

**You:**
- Before sharing a story, check it first!
- Is it from a real news organization?
- Does it sound too good/bad to be true?
- Can you find the info elsewhere?

## Remember

**Good Journalists:**
- Check multiple sources
- Name their sources
- Show facts and numbers
- Admit when they're wrong
- Write boring but true stuff

**Fake News Makers:**
- Use emotions (fear, anger)
- No real sources
- Make wild claims
- Hard to verify
- Sound dramatic

## The Problem

This program catches OBVIOUS fakes really well. But tricky fakes?

"The president had coffee today" - TRUE but meaningless
"The president had coffee with secret agents" - FALSE but sounds real

So always think critically!
