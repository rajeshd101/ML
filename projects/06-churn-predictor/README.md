# Customer Churn Predictor - Who's About to Leave? 👋

## What Does This Do?

Imagine you own a pizza restaurant. Some customers keep coming back. Others stop coming. This program predicts "This customer is about to leave!"

Then you can say "Wait! Here's a discount!" and keep them happy.

## How Does It Work?

**It looks at:**
- How long they've been a customer
- How much they spend each month
- Their contract type (monthly or yearly)
- If they have internet or TV service
- If they complained recently

**Then it predicts:** "This person has 85% chance of leaving"

## The Magic Inside

- **5,000 Customers**: Learned from real telecom customer data
- **38 Clues**: Checks 38 different things about each person
- **Accuracy**: Gets predictions right a lot!

## Example

Customer Profile:
- Been with us 3 months (new!)
- Expensive internet service
- Monthly contract (can leave anytime)
- No support package
- **Prediction: 90% WILL LEAVE** ⚠️

What to do?
- Call them: "Hey! You're new, need help?"
- Offer discount: "Stay 1 year, save $100"
- Add support: "Free tech help for 3 months"

## How to Use It

1. Pick a customer
2. Click "Check Risk"
3. It shows: Risk level (0-100%)
4. Tells you WHY they might leave

## The Numbers That Matter

- **5,000 Customers** = Learned from this many people
- **38 Features** = Different things it checks
- **0-100% Risk Score** = How likely they are to leave

## Why Companies Care

Customer example:
- Cost to get new customer: $500
- Cost to keep old customer: $50
- So if you can predict who's leaving, you can save money!

## Spotting Problems

**Red Flags** 🚩
- Just signed up (month 1-2)
- Expensive service
- Month-to-month contract
- Complained to support
- No extra services

**Green Flags** 🟢
- Been here 2+ years
- Bundled services (internet + TV + phone)
- Long contract (1-2 year)
- Never complained
- Regular spending

## Real World Use

Telecom companies use this to:
- Know who to call and offer deals to
- Improve customer service
- Keep good customers happy
- Save money by preventing cancellations
