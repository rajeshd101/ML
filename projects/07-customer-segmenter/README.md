# Customer Segmenter - Finding Your People 👥

## What Does This Do?

Imagine sorting people at a mall into groups:
- **Rich people** who spend lots
- **Young people** who spend less
- **Window shoppers** who look but don't buy
- **Bargain hunters** who want cheap stuff
- **Middle-class** people with medium spending

This program does exactly that - sorts customers into 5 groups!

## How Does It Work?

**It checks:**
- How old are they
- How much money do they make
- How much do they spend at the mall

**Then it says:** "These 50 people are Group 1 (young and poor), these 45 people are Group 2 (old and rich)", etc.

## The Magic Inside

- **200 Customers**: Analyzed real customer data
- **5 Groups**: Found 5 natural groups of people
- **3 Things it Checks**: Age, income, and spending

## Why This Matters

Different groups = Different marketing!

**For Group 1 (Teens):**
- Show cool new clothes
- Use social media
- Offer trendy stuff

**For Group 3 (Budget Shoppers):**
- Advertise sales
- Coupons and discounts
- Value brands

**For Group 4 (Rich Savers):**
- Premium products
- Exclusive access
- Quality over price

## Real World Use

Mall owners use this to:
- Know who shops where
- Design stores for each group
- Put expensive brands near rich people
- Put discount stores near budget shoppers
- Make better marketing ads

Streaming services use this:
- Netflix: "Are you a horror fan or comedy fan?"
- Spotify: "Do you like pop or rock?"

Instead of trying to sell the same thing to everyone, you:
1. Sort people into groups

3. Give them what they want
